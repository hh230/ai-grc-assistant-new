Every company, regardless of size, should manage compliance risks. ISO 37301 outlines a systematic 
approach to compliance management that helps you stay ahead of legal requirements and regulations, 
prevent significant non-compliance, and improve public image and trust. ISO 37301 provides a 
framework for effective compliance management systems across multiple industries and regions.  
The standard is built on good governance, proportionality, transparency, and sustainability.
WHAT IS A COMPLIANCE MANAGEMENT SYSTEM?
A compliance management system is a set of processes designed to ensure that an organization 
complies with relevant laws, regulations, and codes of conduct. Here are some key elements of a 
compliance management system that ISO 37301 emphasizes:
 · A compliance policy and objectives that align with the company’s strategy and objectives
 · Risk assessment and management processes that identify, assess, and prioritize compliance risks
 · Compliance controls and procedures that prevent or reduce compliance risks
 · Monitoring and reviewing processes that track and evaluate compliance performance  
and effectiveness
 · Continuous improvement processes that review and improve the CMS’s effectiveness

ISO 37301 AND ANTI-CORRUPTION MANAGEMENT
ISO 37001 is the international standard for anti-corruption management systems. It specifies the 
requirements for an anti-bribery management system and helps organizations prevent bribery and 
comply with anti-bribery laws and regulations.  
ISO 37001 and ISO 37301 share many principles and requirements, such as a risk-based approach 
and the PDCA process cycle. Combining these two standards can help companies holistically manage 
compliance and anti-corruption risks.
IMPLEMENTING ISO 37301
Implementing a compliance management system according to ISO 37301 requires a commitment to 
compliance from the top management and the active participation of all employees. Here are some 
tips to help you implement ISO 37301 effectively:
 · Establish a compliance culture that emphasizes integrity, ethics, and accountability
 · Identify and assess compliance risks based on relevant laws and regulations, stakeholders, and 
internal factors
 · Develop and implement policies and controls to prevent and mitigate compliance risks
 · Train and educate employees on the importance of compliance and how to identify and report com -
pliance risks
 · Monitor and review compliance performance and effectiveness regularly
 · Engage external stakeholders, such as suppliers and customers, in your compliance management 
system to improve transparency and trust  
 
THE BENEFITS OF A COMPLIANCE MANAGEMENT SYSTEM
Implementing a compliance management system according to  
ISO 37301 demonstrates that your company takes compliance  
risks seriously and follows the most comprehensive guidelines.  
It increases the confidence that customers, employees,  
and suppliers have in your company.
1. IMPROVED PUBLIC IMAGE AND TRUST
In the event of non-compliance, demonstrating that your 
organization has an effective CMS that meets international 
standards can serve as evidence of due diligence compliance, 
helping to mitigate legal consequences.
2. EVIDENCE OF CORPORATE DUE DILIGENCE COMPLIANCE
ISO 37301 follows the same management system principles and 
structure as other ISO management system standards, such as 
ISO 9001 for quality management. You can easily integrate your 
compliance management system with other management systems 
for more comprehensive management.
3. EASY INTEGRATION WITH OTHER MANAGEMENT SYSTEMS
THE HIGH-LEVEL-STRUCTURE (HLS) OF ISO 37301
The High-Level-Structure (HLS) provides a guideline to help companies structure and unify the requi -
rements of multiple management systems, including health and safety management systems. This 
facilitates the implementation and maintenance of multiple management systems and supports the 
development of integrated management systems.
